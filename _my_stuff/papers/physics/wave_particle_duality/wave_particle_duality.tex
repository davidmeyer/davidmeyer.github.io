\section{Introduction}

\smallskip
\noindent
We know from basic electromagnetism
\cite{wiki:electromagnetic_radiation} that $c = \lambda \nu$,
where $c$ is the speed of light, $\lambda$ is the wavelength, and
$\nu$ is the frequency. Rearranging we get


\bigskip
\begin{equation}
	\nu = \dfrac{c}{\lambda}
	\label{eqn:frequency}
\end{equation}

\bigskip
\noindent
We also know from Planck's work \cite{planck_energy} that
electromagnetic waves have energy $E_{\text{{\tiny P}}}$, where
$E_{\text{{\tiny P}}}$ is defined as


\smallskip
\begin{equation}
	E_{\text{{\tiny P}}} = h \nu
	\label{eqn:planck_energy}
\end{equation}


\bigskip
\noindent
and $h$ is Planck's constant \cite{wiki:plancks_constant}. 
Substituting Equation (\ref{eqn:frequency}) into Equation 
(\ref{eqn:planck_energy}) we get

\medskip
\begin{equation}
	E_{\text{{\tiny P}}} = h \dfrac{c}{\lambda}
	\label{eqn:planck_c_over_lambda}
\end{equation}

\medskip
\noindent
In addition, we know from Einstein \cite{wiki:mass_energy_equivalence}
that the energy of a mass $m$, call it $E_{\text{{\tiny E}}}$, is


\begin{equation}
	E_{\text{{\tiny E}}} = mc^2
	\label{eqn:E=mc^2}
\end{equation}

\bigskip
\noindent
What de Broglie \cite{wiki:de_broglie} realized was that matter
must also behave like a wave\footnote{Einstein had already shown
that an electromagnetic wave can behave like a particle
\cite{wiki:photoelectric_effect}.} (aka wave-particle duality
\cite{wiki:wave_particle_duality}) and so Equation
(\ref{eqn:planck_c_over_lambda}) must equal Equation
(\ref{eqn:E=mc^2}). This implies that matter also has 
a wavelength: $\lambda =
\dfrac{h}{\mathbf{p}}$, where $\mathbf{p}$ is the momentum
($\mathbf{p} = m \mathbf{v}$). 

\bigskip
\noindent
$\lambda$ is called the \emph{de Broglie wavelength}. 
Here's one way to think about where it comes from:

\begin{equation*}
\begin{array}{llll}
h \dfrac{c}{\lambda}
&=& mc^2	
			&\hspace{5em} \mathrel{\#} \text{de Broglie's insight: 
				$E_{\text{{\tiny P}}} = E_{\text{{\tiny E}}}$ (aka 
				wave-particle duality \cite{wiki:matter_wave})} \\
[12pt]
&\Rightarrow& \dfrac{h}{\lambda} = m c
			&\hspace{5em} \mathrel{\#} \text{cancel $c$}\\
[12pt]
&\Rightarrow& \dfrac{h}{\lambda} = m \mathbf{v}
			&\hspace{5em} \mathrel{\#} \mathbf{v} = c \\
[12pt]
&\Rightarrow& \lambda = \dfrac{h}{m \mathbf{v}}
			&\hspace{5em} \mathrel{\#} \text{solve for $\lambda$}\\
[12pt]
&\Rightarrow& \lambda = \dfrac{h}{\mathbf{p}}
			&\hspace{5em} \mathrel{\#} \mathbf{p} = m \mathbf{v} \\
[12pt] 
\end{array}
\end{equation*}
