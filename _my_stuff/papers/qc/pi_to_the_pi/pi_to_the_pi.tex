\title{Is There A Closed Form Expression For $\pi^\pi$?}
\author{David Meyer \\ \href{mailto:dmm@1-4-5.net}{dmm@1-4-5.net}}

\large
\noindent
How can we find a closed form expression for $\pi^\pi$? The first thing
we might do is to consider what $x^x$ (and so $\pi^\pi$) might mean. 
Well, in general $a^x$ is defined as $e^{x \ln a}$ for positive $a$ since 

\bigskip
\begin{equation*}
\begin{array}{llll}
\ln a^x 
&=& x \ln a 									&\qquad \qquad \mathrel{\#} \text{power rule for logarithms} \\
[10pt]
&\Rightarrow& e^{\ln a^x} = e^{x \ln a}			&\qquad \qquad \mathrel{\#} \text{exponentiate both sides} \\
[10pt]
&\Rightarrow& a^x = e^{x \ln a}					&\qquad \qquad \mathrel{\#} e^{\ln x} = x\\
\end{array}
\end{equation*}

\bigskip
\noindent
Using the same reasoning for positive $x$ we have $x^x = e^{x \ln x}$. If we 
set $x = \pi$ then we get 

\begin{equation}
{\displaystyle \pi^{\pi} = e^{\pi \ln \pi}}
\label{eqn:pi_to_the_pi}
\end{equation}

\bigskip
\noindent
All good, but what is $e^{\pi \ln \pi}$? We can use a Maclaurin series 
to evaluate this expression as follows:

\medskip
\begin{equation*}
\begin{array}{llll}
e^x
&=& {\displaystyle \sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{x^n}{n!}}
	&\qquad \qquad \qquad \mathrel{\#} \text{Maclaurin (Taylor) series for $e^x$} \\
[20pt]
&\Rightarrow& {\displaystyle e^{\pi \ln \pi} = \sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{(\pi \ln\pi)^n}{n!}}	
		&\qquad \qquad \qquad \mathrel{\#} \text{set $x = \pi \ln \pi$} \\
[20pt]
&\Rightarrow& {\displaystyle e^{\pi \ln \pi} =\sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{\pi^n \ln^n\pi}{n!}} 		
	&\qquad \qquad \qquad \mathrel{\#} \text{simplify} \\
[20pt]
&\Rightarrow& {\displaystyle \pi^\pi = \sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{\pi^n \ln^n\pi}{n!}}													
	&\qquad \qquad \qquad \mathrel{\#} \text{$e^{\pi \ln \pi} = \pi^\pi$ (Equation (\ref{eqn:pi_to_the_pi}))}
 
\end{array}
\end{equation*}

\bigskip
\noindent
So we see the cool result that

{\Large
\begin{equation*}
{\mathlarger \pi^\pi = \sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{\pi^n \ln^n\pi}{n!}}
\end{equation*}}
