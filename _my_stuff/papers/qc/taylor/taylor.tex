\section{Taylor Inequality}
Suppose the Taylor polynomial $T(x) = T_n(x) + R_n(x)$, where $T_n(x) =  \sum\limits _{i=0}^{n}{\frac {f^{(i)}(a)}{i!}}(x-a)^{i}$
and  $R_n(x) =  \sum\limits _{i=n}^{\infty}{\frac {f^{(i)}(a)}{i!}}(x-a)^{i}$. 

\bigskip
\noindent
Then if $|f^{(n)}| \leq M$ for $|x - a| \leq d$ then the remainder $R_n(x)$ of the
Taylor series satisfies the inequality

\bigskip
\begin{equation*}
|R_n(x)|  \leq \frac{M}{(n+1)!} |x -a|^{n+1}
\end{equation*}

\bigskip
\noindent
for $|x-a| \leq d$, where $M$ and $d$ are some constants.
