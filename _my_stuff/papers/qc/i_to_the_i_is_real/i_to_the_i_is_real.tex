\title {Is $i^i$ a real number and if so, what does it equal?}
\author{David Meyer \\ dmm@\{1-4-5.net,uoregon.edu\}}

\noindent
To answer this question, consider Euler's formula \cite{notes:eulers_formula,wiki:eulers_formula} evaluated at $\frac{\pi}{2}$:


{\Large
\begin{equation*}
\begin{array}{lllll}
e^{ix}
&=& \cos x + i \sin x                                                                                                   &\qquad \qquad \qquad  \mathrel{\#} \text{Euler's formula}   \\ 
&\Rightarrow& e^{i \frac{\pi}{2}}  = \cos \frac{\pi}{2} + i \sin \frac{\pi}{2}                    &\qquad \qquad  \qquad  \mathrel{\#} \text{set $x = \frac{\pi}{2}$}                                            \\
&\Rightarrow& e^{i \frac{\pi}{2}}  = 0 +  i \cdot 1                                                       &\qquad \qquad  \qquad  \mathrel{\#} \cos \frac{\pi}{2} = 0 \text{ and} \sin \frac{\pi}{2} = 1       \\
&\Rightarrow& e^{i \frac{\pi}{2}}  =  i                                                                         &\qquad \qquad  \qquad  \mathrel{\#} \text{simplify}                                                                  \\
&\Rightarrow& (e^{i \frac{\pi}{2}})^i =  i^i                                                                   &\qquad \qquad  \qquad  \mathrel{\#} \text{raise both sides to $i$}                                           \\
&\Rightarrow& e^{\frac{i^2 \pi}{2}} =  i^i                                                                    &\qquad \qquad  \qquad  \mathrel{\#}  (x^m)^n = x^{mn}                                                           \\
&\Rightarrow& e^{- \frac {\pi}{2}} =  i^i                                                                      &\qquad \qquad  \qquad  \mathrel{\#} i^2 = -1                                                                            \\
&\Rightarrow& e^{- \frac {\pi}{2}} \in \mathbb{R} \Rightarrow i^i \in \mathbb{R}       &\qquad \qquad  \qquad  \mathrel{\#} \text{$i^i$ is a real number}                                        
\end{array}
\end{equation*}
}

\medskip
\bigskip
\noindent
Ok, $i^i \in \mathbb{R}$, but what does $i^i$ equal? Well, we saw that $e^{- \frac {\pi}{2}} =  i^i$. So

\bigskip
\begin{equation*}
i^i = e^{- \frac {\pi}{2}} \approx 0.20788
\end{equation*}
